General Non-Fiction:

Gold in the Water - PH Mullen Jr.
The book that started it all, for me. The story of the Santa Clara Swim Club's 2000 Olympic push, of ordinary men racked by Olympic Fever. A peerless example of sports writing at its best. Tom Wilkens, Kurt Grote, and coach Dick Jochums are characters that have stuck with me to this day, for the depth and grit and humanity that Mullen lays bare as he chronicles their quests for Olympic glory

Encounters with the Archdruid – John McPhee

Three men have three fateful encounters with David Brower, prominent mountaineer turned militant conservationist. All three wish to develop land in some way, for minining or electric power or tourism, and McPhee masterfully documents as Brower plays interlocutor and skeptic

History and Politics:

Annals of the Former World - John McPhee
This is really three books in one: 1) The geologic history of the land that is now the United States 2) The evolution of geologic study in America 3) McPhee's personal discovery and interaction with the first two. This book will leave you yearning to drop whatever you're doing, get on I-80 and stare at rock cuts

Ho Chi Minh: A Life – William Duiker

A stunning, illuminating tome on the life of Ho Chi Minh. Born to a confucian scholar, Uncle Ho sojourned in Europe, the United States, and the Soviet Union before making his way back to Vietnam where he lead the Viet Minh independence movement. After repelling Japanese and French imperial intrusions, Ho Chi Minh was made President of the North Vietnamese government. A revolutionary, a liberator, a poet and conciliator, a humble pragmatist and champion of the people - Duiker paints an enthralling image of the misunderstood world leader
